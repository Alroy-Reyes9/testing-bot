What Is Pickleball?

🎾

Tennis × Ping-Pong

Played on a badminton-sized court with a perforated plastic ball and solid paddles. Singles or doubles — your choice.

🏸

The Kitchen Rule

The 7-foot "kitchen" zone near the net. You can't volley inside it — this keeps the game strategic and fair for all skill levels.

💪

Low Impact, High Fun

Easy on joints but still a great workout. Rallying burns 300–500 calories an hour while you're too busy having fun to notice.

🤝

Built for Community

Most games are social drop-in. No team required — just show up, grab a paddle, and meet your new friends on the court.

What You'll Need

🏓

Paddle

Composite or graphite face, polymer honeycomb core. Lightweight — 7–9 oz. Beginner paddles start around $30.

Essential
⚪

Ball

Perforated plastic ball with 26–40 holes. Indoor balls are lighter; outdoor balls are heavier to handle wind.

Essential
👟

Shoes

Court shoes with good lateral support. Tennis or volleyball shoes work great. No running shoes — they lack side grip.

Recommended
🕶️

Accessories

Overgrip for sweat control, polarized sunglasses for outdoor play, and a water bottle — you'll need it!

Quick Rules

1
Serve Underhand

Diagonal serve, below the waist. Ball must bounce once before returning (two-bounce rule).

2
The Two-Bounce Rule

Ball must bounce once on each side before anyone can volley (hit in the air).

3
Stay Out of the Kitchen

No volleying while standing in or touching the 7-foot non-volley zone.

4
Win by 2

Games are played to 11 points. You must win by 2. Switch sides when the sum of scores equals 6 or 8.

5
Doubles Rules

Each player on a team serves before the serve passes to the other team. Score is called as 3 numbers.

6
Side Out

Only the serving team can score. If the receiving team wins the rally, they get the serve (side out).

Find a Court

Pickleball courts are everywhere — parks, rec centers, schools, churches. Here's how to find one near you.

📍 Parks & Rec

Most city parks now have dedicated pickleball courts or shared tennis courts with pickleball lines. Check your local parks department website.

📱 Apps

Use Pickleball App, PicklePlay, or CourtFinder to see real-time availability and book courts near you.

🏠 Indoor Clubs

Dedicated pickleball clubs are booming. Many offer day passes, rentals, and beginner clinics. Search "indoor pickleball near me."

🎯 Pro tip: Show up during "open play" hours — people love helping new players. Just bring a paddle and ask to play!
